About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Shirts Group specializing in leveraging molecular dynamics simulations to explore protein binding interactions. My research blends bioengineering and computational chemistry to study complex protein interactions in diverse systems as well as develop innovative tools and methodologies that accelerate conformational sampling in fleible protein systems. I am passionate about applying cutting-edge computational techniques to solve complex biological problems, particularly in the context of enzyme-substrate dynamics and protein interactions. With a strong foundation in molecular simulations, alchemical free energy, and enhanced sampling methods, I strive to continue contributing to better understanding complex biological process and imporving how these systems are studied in the future. In my free time, I love exploring the beautiful Rocky Mountains with my husband and our two dogs, expressing my creativity through sewing, knitting, and crocheting, and baking homemade sweet treats.

Projects

MT-REXEE

Along with collaborators, I have developed and implementated of a novel enhanced sampling method for alchemical relative free energy caluclations. This method allows for parallel iterative relative free energy calculations to share conformational information to enhance sampling. This method targets large flexible transformations which would otherwise be difficult to achieve adequate conformational sampling to produce relaible free energy estimates.

Study of Allosteric Inhibiton in PTP1B

Allosteric inhibition is a popular mechanism of choice for inhibiting "undruggable" proteins which have high active site conservation like that of PTP1B. We used novel inhibitors Amorphadiene and α-bisabolene to investigate the mechanism of allosteric inhibition in both PTP1B and TCPTP to inform future inhibitor design.
