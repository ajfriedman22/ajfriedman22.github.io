About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Shirts Group specializing in leveraging molecular dynamics simulations to explore protein binding interactions. My research blends bioengineering and computational chemistry to study complex protein interactions in diverse systems as well as develop innovative tools and methodologies that accelerate conformational sampling in flexible protein systems. I am passionate about applying cutting-edge computational techniques to solve complex biological problems, particularly in the context of enzyme-substrate dynamics and protein interactions. With a strong foundation in molecular simulations, alchemical free energy, and enhanced sampling methods, I strive to continue contributing to better understanding complex biological process and improving how these systems are studied in the future. In my free time, I love exploring the beautiful Rocky Mountains with my husband and our two dogs, expressing my creativity through sewing, knitting, and crocheting, and baking homemade sweet treats.

Projects

MT-REXEE

Along with collaborators, I have developed and implementated of a novel enhanced sampling method for alchemical relative free energy caluclations. This method allows for parallel iterative relative free energy calculations to share conformational information to enhance sampling. This method targets large flexible transformations which would otherwise be difficult to achieve adequate conformational sampling to produce relaible free energy estimates.

GitHub Paper

Open Force Field Protein Force Field Development

The Open Force Field (OpenFF) Initiative is a non-profit of industry and academic researchers who work together to produce improved open-source biomolecular force fields. I have been involved primarily in extending OpenFF's existing small molecule force field to proteins creating a fully self-consistant force field.

Website GitHub

PYR1-based Biosensor Design

This project focused on the effect of mutations to tune the affinity of a biosensor scaffold for different small molecules. During the course of this project I created an automated pipline for generating small molecule conformers using temperrature replica exchange molecular dynamics.

GitHub Paper

Substrate Specificity in Fab Enzymes

Using molecular dynamics (MD) simulations to elude the mechanism by which the ketoacyl synthase FabF and FabB elongates acyl chains for fatty acid synthesis and FabG performs reduction reactions on acyl products. This project focuses on the dependancy of the inter-protein interface on substrate specificity.

GitHub

ML/MD Enhanced Sampling to Study Flexible SH2 Binding Interfaces

Exploring the use of generative machine learning tools (like AlphaFold, ESMfold, etc.) for the generation of an ensemble of protein configurations. These protein configurations could then be used as initial configurations for MD simulations. Our inital test system is utilizing SH2-peptide binding complexes.

Study of Allosteric Inhibiton in PTP1B

Allosteric inhibition is a popular mechanism of choice for inhibiting "undruggable" proteins which have high active site conservation like that of PTP1B. We used novel inhibitors Amorphadiene and α-bisabolene to investigate the mechanism of allosteric inhibition in both PTP1B and TCPTP to inform future inhibitor design.
